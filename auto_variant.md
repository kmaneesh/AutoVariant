SOP for WES Variant Details

Step1: Open Ion Reporter, Search the sample then search the Gene click on + sign and take information of zygosity, variant effect, coding region, gene name, locus, and coverage.
E.g.: “A hemizygous missense variant c.3293G>A in COL4A5 gene (chrX:107898607; Depth:20x) was detected.”
Step2: Write an Amino Acid change from ion reporter
E.g.: “This variant (p.Gly1098Asp) replaces glycine, which is neutral and non-polar, with aspartic acid, which is acidic and polar, at 1098th amino acid position.”
Step3: Open ClinVar database, Franklin Genoox, VarSome database and online information of the variant then add how the variant change and protein change affect the gene and protein. Also add the evidence or literature from PubMed or any population database if the same variant available.
E.g.: “This variant disrupts the triple helix domain of COL4A5. Glycine residues within the Gly-Xaa-Yaa repeats of the triple helix domain are required for the structure and stability of fibrillar collagens (PMID: 7695699, 8218237, 19344236). In COL4A5, missense variants at these glycine residues are significantly enriched in individuals with disease (PMID: 23720012, 27627812) compared to the general population (ExAC).”
Step4: Open Franklin Genoox database then click on variant assessment then check population database frequencies gnomAD (Max) and ExAc of this variant if available or reported.
E.g.: “This variant has minor allele frequency of 0.0196% and 0.0025% in gnomAD (max) and ExAC database respectively.”
If not available: “” The variant is absent from major population databases such as 1000 Genomes, ExAC, topMed, and gnomAD, suggesting it is either extremely rare or potentially novel.””
Step5: Open ClinVar database and add the variation ID if available or reported.
E.g.: “The variant has been reported in clinvar database (Variation ID: 634650).” 
If not available: “” The variant has not been reported in clinvar database.””
Step6: Open Franklin Genoox database then click on variant assessment then check In-silico data of SIFT, Revel, MT, Polyphen-2, CADD and MVP whether its deleterious or not.
E.g.: “In-silico prediction tools suggest a deleterious effect by SIFT, MT, CADD, REVEL and MVP.” 
If not deleterious: “” In-silico prediction tools suggest an uncertain effect by SIFT, MT, CADD, REVEL and MVP.””
Note: “”” In-silico prediction tools frameshift variants, Splice-site variants and null variants are classified as deleterious, since they disrupt the open reading frame and typically lead to nonsense-mediated mRNA decay or production of a nonfunctional truncated protein.
Step7: Check the Exomiser classification whether its Pathogenic, likely pathogenic or uncertain significance according to ACMG-AMP guidelines.
Pathogenic: “Based on the aforementioned evidence, this variant is classified as pathogenic according to the ACMG AMP guidelines.” 
Likely pathogenic: “” Based on the aforementioned evidence, this variant is classified as likely pathogenic according to the ACMG AMP guidelines.””
Uncertain significance: “”” Based on the aforementioned evidence, this variant is classified as variant of uncertain significance according to the ACMG AMP guidelines.”””
